# Project Retrospective - Enrich Apparel

## What Went Well
- The creation of the website and the final result left me satisfied with my work.
- The scope of the project was well defined from the start, helping me stay aligned in my scope.
- The documentation of the project aligned with the principles of project management.
- All of the website pages displayed consistent branding and information. 

## What Went Wrong
- I did not like having no pictures for the products, but I did not just want to steal one from the internet. This caused my website quality to suffer as a result.
- The time management in this project could have been much better. Several aspects of this project took longer than expected and could have been more polished with a greater time commitment from me.
- My lack of familiarity with Git caused several issues and led to excess stress and a lack of production, with time being lost trying to resolve those issues. 

## What Would Be Done Differently
- I would want to add some basic product images, maybe something I created myself, or find another alternative to make my website better.
- I would develop a deeper understanding of GitHub to become more familiar and resolve the issues I faced more quickly.
- The marketing simulation for the project could be more advanced by having sample posts or pictures on the website itself.
- The design of the website could be more polished if I had a better understanding of style.css and my two HTML websites.
